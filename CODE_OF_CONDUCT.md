# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want Cairn community to be open, useful, and respectful for everyone.
Contributors, maintainers, and participants should act in ways that make
technical collaboration possible without harassment or exclusion.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful in technical disagreement.
- Focus feedback on code, docs, design, and behavior.
- Assume good intent, but be precise when something is unclear or broken.
- Share context, repro steps, and evidence when reporting issues.
- Help keep discussion accessible for newcomers.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory language or exclusionary behavior
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or bad-faith escalation
- Publishing private information without consent
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions,
and other project spaces where Cairn community interaction happens.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, and
other contributions that violate this code of conduct.

For serious or repeated problems, maintainers may temporarily or permanently
restrict participation.

## Reporting

Report conduct issues privately to project maintainers through GitHub or other
maintainer-provided contact channels. Reports should include links, screenshots,
or concrete context when possible.

Maintainers will review reports and respond with reasonable care and discretion.
